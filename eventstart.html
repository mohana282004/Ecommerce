Music notation can be understood through a few key elements. Notes are represented by symbols that indicate their pitch and duration. For instance, a whole note looks like a hollow circle and lasts for four beats, while a quarter note, which is a filled circle with a stem, lasts for just one beat. The basic notes in Western music are C, D, E, F, G, A, and B, and these can be sharp (#) or flat (b) to alter their pitch. Time signatures like 4/4 or 3/4 dictate the number of beats in each measure, helping to organize the rhythm of a piece. Understanding these basics helps in reading and playing music effectively.
